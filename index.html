The Beginning

Aurelius Augustinus Hipponensis better known as St. Augustine was born in 354, in Thagaste, North Africa to a christian mother and a pagan father who actually converted to christianity on his deathbed
His family ancestery likely included latins, berbers, and phoenecians. He considored himself to be a punic. His family name of Aurelius suggests his fathr's ancestors were freedmen of the group Aurelia, given full roman citizenship by the Edict of Caracalla in 212. By the time of Augustine's birth his famiyl had likely been Roman for at least a century. His family were honestiores, upperclass of Roman Citizens known as honorable men (The family had repect but likely not wealth). As a result he recieved the best education and his first language was probably latin. Auguetine showed early promise in school so his parents sent him to Madaurus at the age of eleven for education where he provent himself extremly intelligent. He recalled his home life as happy and his school life less so.

Conversion

Up to this point Augustine had always considored christianty lacking in logic but Bishop Andrews of Milan convinced him otherwise. His skilled oration together with his helping Augustine to understand christianity in a new light caused Augustine to make a break with Mechanism his previous belief and considor christianity. In fall of 386 he had a conversion experience and withdrew with a group of like minded friends for the winter to an isolated place. That whole winter they discussed the faith and on easter of next year he was baptized by Bishop Ambrose.

Becoming bishop

In 389 Augustine came back to Thagaste, he lived on his family estate in a somewhat monastic community. In 391 he visited the city of hippo Regius in order to start a monastary but ended up being drafted into the priesthood and in 395 became the bishop of Hippo where he continued to write, he also took care of his son who wasborn out of wedlock. He spent the next 35 years preaching, celebrating mass, resolving local disputes, and ministering to his congregation. He continued to write, and he became famous throughout the Christian world for his role in several controversies.

Adulthood

Augustine's oration skills would come in handy later during controversies within the church. Donatists a christian sect were attacking Catholics who they believed to be so illegitamently, his work helped keep the christian world together as well as the congregation at Hippo.

Memory

Today Augustine is remembered as a doctor of the church. His work would influence medieval thinking for centuries to come, he over 300 seremons, 500 letters, and numerous other works. His City of God and Confessions are huge masterpieces which are still very realavent today. He is a standing monumentum that anyone can become a saint.
